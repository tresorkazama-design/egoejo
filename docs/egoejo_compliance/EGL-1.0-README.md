## Overview

The **EGOEJO Governance License (EGL-1.0)** is an open governance license designed to ensure that content maintains its educational integrity, non-commercial nature, and institutional compliance while allowing free use, modification, and distribution.

- ✅ Viral governance clause (derivative works must maintain compliance)

## How to Use This License

### For Content Creators

1. **Choose EGL-1.0**: If you want your content to be freely usable while maintaining governance compliance.

2. **Add License Notice**: Include the following in your content:
   ```
   This work is licensed under the EGOEJO Governance License (EGL) Version 1.0.
   To view a copy of this license, visit: [URL to EGL-1.0]
   ```

3. **Maintain Governance Compliance**: Ensure your content:
   - Has no financial language, monetary symbols, or financial promises
   - Follows valid publication workflow (if published)
   - Has source attribution
   - Has license attribution

4. **Use the Label**: If compliant, you can use the "EGOEJO Compliant" label.

### For Content Users

1. **Read the License**: Understand the terms, especially Governance Compliance requirements.

2. **Attribution**: Give appropriate credit to the original author and source.

3. **License Notice**: Include a copy of the license with any distribution.

4. **Viral Governance**: If you create derivative works, they must also be licensed under EGL-1.0 or a compatible license.

5. **Maintain Compliance**: Ensure derivative works maintain Governance Compliance.

## Governance Compliance Requirements

### Mandatory Requirements

All content licensed under EGL-1.0 must:

1. ❌ **No Financial Language**: No ROI, profit, rentability, financial gain, etc.
2. ❌ **No Monetary Symbols**: No €, $, EUR, USD, GBP (except in strictly informative contexts)
3. ❌ **No Financial Promises**: No promises of financial return, guarantee, or assurance
4. ✅ **Valid Workflow**: If published, must follow draft → pending → published workflow
5. ✅ **Source Attribution**: Must have identifiable source (URL, file, reference)
6. ✅ **License Attribution**: Must have explicit license (Creative Commons, Public Domain, etc.)

### Recommended Requirements

1. ⚠️ **Audit Trail**: Should have audit log of actions
2. ⚠️ **Publication Traceability**: Should record author, date, and history

## Automatic Revocation

The License and Label are **automatically revoked** if:

- ❌ Violation of Governance Compliance (mandatory requirements)
- ❌ Financial exploitation or commercial misrepresentation
- ❌ Workflow bypass (direct publication without validation)
- ❌ License incompatibility (derivative works under incompatible license)

**Effect of Revocation:**
- Loss of License permissions
- Loss of "EGOEJO Compliant" label
- Prohibition of distribution under EGL-1.0
- Remediation period (typically 30 days) to correct violations

## Viral Governance Clause

**Key Principle**: Derivative works must maintain Governance Compliance.

**Requirements:**
- Derivative works must be licensed under EGL-1.0 or compatible license
- Derivative works must maintain Governance Compliance
- Derivative works must maintain source attribution (original + intermediate)
- Derivative works must maintain license attribution

**Incompatible Licenses:**
- Any license that permits financial exploitation
- Any license that does not require source attribution
- Any license that does not maintain Governance Compliance

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: Can I use EGL-1.0 for commercial purposes?

**A**: Yes, but you must maintain Governance Compliance. No financial promises, monetary symbols, or commercial misrepresentation.

### Q2: What happens if I violate Governance Compliance?

**A**: The License and Label are automatically revoked. You lose all permissions and must correct violations to restore compliance.

### Q3: Can I create derivative works under a different license?

**A**: Only if the license is compatible with EGL-1.0 and maintains Governance Compliance. Otherwise, automatic revocation applies.

### Q4: Is EGL-1.0 compatible with Creative Commons?

**A**: Yes, when Governance Compliance is maintained. EGL-1.0 is compatible with CC BY, CC BY-SA, CC BY-NC, CC BY-NC-SA, and CC0.

### Q5: How do I know if my content is compliant?

**A**: Use the compliance tests and API endpoints. See the Editorial Constitution for detailed requirements.

### Q6: Can I use the "EGOEJO Compliant" label?

**A**: Yes, if your content is licensed under EGL-1.0 and maintains Governance Compliance. The label is automatically granted and can be automatically revoked.
